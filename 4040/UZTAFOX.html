UZTAFOX

UZLAPFOX is the official language of KROMZZAKA SSUZOΠKUXA. It was introduced shortly after it's founding to further national identity and facilitate communication between citizen of different backgrounds as well as further isolating the population from foreign influence. The stated goals of UPAPFOX is to be simple and easy to learn in nature while still being able to support technical language and jargon of various types as well as common/casual language. This was done by having a small inventory of atomic, short words that can build up to more complex concepts.

The letters are mostly capitalized latin letters with 2 exceptions from the cyrillic or greek alphabet. Double letters (SS, ZZ, II, UU, AA) often get their own symbol with one or more lines drawn as a double line, similarly to how sets of numbers (like ℝ) are written. For latinization, Γ and Π can be written with the lower case letters r and g respectively. Arabic numerals are used for digits, and mathematical notation remains mostly unchanged. Grammar Pronouns MA, TA and XA are singular pronouns in the 1st, 2nd and 3rd person (I, you, he/she/it). plural pronouns are composed depending on which people are involved. e.g. TAXA is involving the one or more people 2nd person and one or more people 3rd person (you as in you people and these other people or you and that other guy) TAA and XAA refer to a group of multiple people in the 2nd person and multiple people in the 3rd person respectively. Tense

Tense is indicated by keywords placed anywhere in the sentence. (Leading Tense keywords can place emphasis on the tense). These are FATO for future tenses, UFATO for prior tenses and XOKO for the current one. These keywords can be stacked to describe events that took place in relation to other events (e.g. UFATUFATFATO KOΓI -> I had been planning to go later). If the tense keyword is omitted, the tense of the previous sentence or sub sentence is implied, or XOKU if it is the first sentence.

Special Verbs

ASI is a special verb to use to associate a subject with a verb (MA ASI XOKZAKU -> I am bored). In informal language, ASI (and sometimes the subject) often gets omitted. ASI and UASI (the negation of ASI) can be used as yes and no.

Word Composition

UZLAPFOX has a relatively small number of root words that can be chained together to form different concepts. The suffix of a word then indicates it's role.

-A -> noun
-I -> verb
-U -> adjective
-O -> adverb

In addition U- as a prefix indicates a negation
A word structure could be U - root - root - (U - root) - A

Examples

UMILZAKA (Sadness) -> U - MIL - ZAK - A (not - nice - feeling - [Noun])
SMIΠKOKMAΠU (Green) -> SMIΠ - KOK - MAΠ - U (life - matter - color - [Adjective])
KUZZKOΓFOTA (Aluminium) -> KUZZ - KOΓ - FOT (sky - travel - metal - [Noun]) or (aircraft - metal)
UUMINA (Infinity) -> U - U - MIN - A (not - not - number - [Noun]) or rather (anti - zero)

Numbers

MIN- is the number/count root word, so numbers themselves end in -MINA while a count is ended in -MINO. Zero is UMINA (not - amount). Further numbers get written in decimal notation like so: digit - order - digit - order - digit - order where a digit is one of MO, TO, XO, PO, ZO, DO, KO, GO, NO from 1 to 9 and is omitted for 0. orders are of type OK, OOK, MIL for 10, 100 and 1000. the order is omitted for 1 and takes form of MI - n - L for higher orders where n is the amount of orders above 1000.
